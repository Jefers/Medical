Persistent Cough Diagnosis Tree

This is for educational purposes only. Consult a doctor for any medical concerns.

Question 1: Do you have any red-flag symptoms? (e.g., coughing up blood, unexplained weight loss, night sweats, prolonged fever)

Likely serious cause: These symptoms could indicate conditions like infection, cancer, or other urgent issues. Seek immediate medical attention.

Question 2: Are you a current smoker or have a significant history of smoking?

Likely cause: Smoking-related, such as chronic bronchitis or COPD. Quit smoking and see a doctor for evaluation (e.g., spirometry).

Question 3: Are you taking ACE inhibitor medications? (e.g., lisinopril, enalapril for blood pressure)

Likely cause: Medication side effect. Discuss switching medications with your doctor.

Question 4: Do you have symptoms of allergies or postnasal drip? (e.g., runny nose, sneezing, frequent throat clearing)

Likely cause: Upper airway cough syndrome (postnasal drip). Try antihistamines/decongestants and see a doctor if no improvement.

Question 5: Do you experience wheezing, shortness of breath, or symptoms worsening at night/with exercise?

Likely cause: Asthma. See a doctor for lung function tests and possible inhalers.

Question 6: Do you have heartburn, regurgitation, or symptoms worsening when lying down?

Likely cause: Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD). Try antacids or PPIs and consult a doctor.

Question 7: Did your cough start after a recent upper respiratory infection (e.g., cold or flu)?

Likely cause: Post-viral cough. It may resolve on its own, but see a doctor if it persists beyond 8 weeks.

Likely cause: Less common issues (e.g., infections, environmental irritants, or other lung/heart conditions). See a specialist for further tests like CT scan or bronchoscopy.
